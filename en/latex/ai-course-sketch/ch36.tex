\chapter{Limits and Future Directions}

\section{Robustness and Uncertainty}

Modern machine learning systems achieve impressive performance, yet they remain sensitive to perturbations and uncertainty.

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Robustness.}  
A model is robust if its predictions remain stable under small changes to input:
\[
\|x - x'\| \text{ small} \;\Rightarrow\; f(x) \approx f(x').
\]

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Adversarial examples.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Carefully crafted perturbations can significantly alter predictions
    \item Often imperceptible to humans
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Distribution shift.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Training and test distributions may differ
    \item Performance can degrade under new conditions
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Uncertainty estimation.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Aleatoric uncertainty:} inherent noise in data
    \item \textbf{Epistemic uncertainty:} uncertainty due to limited knowledge
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Approaches.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Bayesian methods
    \item Ensemble models
    \item Calibration techniques
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Insight.}  
Reliable AI systems must quantify and manage uncertainty in addition to making accurate predictions.

\section{Failure Modes of Modern AI}

Despite strong empirical performance, modern models exhibit systematic failure modes.

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Common issues.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Overconfidence in incorrect predictions
    \item Sensitivity to spurious correlations
    \item Lack of robustness to rare or out-of-distribution inputs
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Data dependence.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Models inherit biases present in training data
    \item Performance reflects data distribution rather than true understanding
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Generalization limits.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Difficulty extrapolating beyond training regime
    \item Challenges with compositional reasoning
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Scaling limitations.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Increasing size does not solve all problems
    \item Some failures persist even at large scale
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Insight.}  
Understanding failure modes is essential for improving reliability and guiding future research.

\section{Interpretability}

Interpretability seeks to understand how and why models make predictions.

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Motivation.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Trust and transparency
    \item Debugging and model improvement
    \item Scientific understanding
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Types of interpretability.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Feature attribution:} importance of inputs
    \item \textbf{Representation analysis:} structure of learned features
    \item \textbf{Model simplification:} approximating complex models with simpler ones
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Challenges.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item High-dimensional representations
    \item Nonlinear interactions
    \item Lack of unique explanations
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Tradeoffs.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Accuracy vs interpretability
    \item Simplicity vs fidelity
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Insight.}  
Interpretability is not a single property, but a collection of approaches to understanding complex models.
